Jeremy Irons

Born in the Isle of Wight in England in 1948.

Like many great screen actors Jeremy trod the boards for many years, starting his acting career performing classic Shakespearean plays.

His first work for television was a part in the 1981 series for ITV 'Brideshead Revisited', for which he received an Emmy nomination. He has however gone on to win many major awards, including an Oscar for best actor for his role in 'Reversal of Fortune' in 1990 and an Emmy Award for his role in 'Elizabeth I'.

A very talented actor equally at home on screen or stage, he even does voice parts for animations, such as the villian in 'The Lion King', and the voice of a Bar Rag in an episode of 'The Simpsons'!

The authors favourite role was his part in the 1992 film 'Damage' where he acted alongside Juliette Binoche as a cabinet minister who becomes involved in a dangerous love affair.
